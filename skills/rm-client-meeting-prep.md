# RM Client Meeting Preparation Framework

## Purpose

This skill governs how Claude prepares meeting briefs for relationship managers. The objective is to convert raw portfolio and CRM data into a scannable, discussion-ready document — not a data dump. Everything produced should serve the RM's conversation, not replace it.

---

## Core Principle

**Relevance over completeness.** An RM preparing for a client meeting needs 3–5 strong talking points, not 20 observations. Prioritize what is materially different, client-relevant, or actionable since the last interaction. Leave the rest out.

---

## Meeting Prep Phases

Process client meeting prep in four sequential phases:

### Phase 1 — Context

Establish the frame before generating any observations.

- Who is the client? (segment, risk profile, relationship tenure, AUM tier)
- When did they last meet or interact with their RM?
- What was discussed or actioned at the last meeting?
- What is the meeting purpose? (scheduled review, product discussion, client-initiated, etc.)
- What is the current market backdrop relevant to this client's holdings?

This phase determines the lens for everything that follows. Do not skip it, even if data is sparse.

### Phase 2 — Observations

Scan portfolio and interaction data to identify what is notable. Apply the **"so what" filter**: only surface observations that a client would reasonably care about or an RM would reasonably act on.

**Portfolio observations to look for:**
- Positions up or down significantly since last meeting (materiality threshold: >5% move for larger positions, >10% for smaller ones)
- Positions approaching or breaching concentration limits
- Holdings with upcoming corporate events (earnings, dividends, AGMs)
- Holdings with analyst rating or house view changes
- Cash drag or idle allocation that may need discussion
- Unrealized gains or losses with potential tax relevance (year-end proximity, client preference)

**Relationship observations to look for:**
- Pending follow-ups or commitments from last meeting
- Recent client communications that signaled concerns or interest
- Life events or mandate changes logged in CRM
- Inactivity or reduced engagement signals

### Phase 3 — Topics

Convert observations from Phase 2 into 3–5 talking points. Each talking point must:

1. Reference a specific observation (not a generic statement)
2. Be framed from the client's perspective (why does this matter to them?)
3. Indicate whether it is **informational**, **requires discussion**, or **requires a decision**

**What makes a good talking point:**
- Tied to something that changed, is changing, or requires attention
- Specific enough to prompt a real conversation
- Proportionate to the client's likely level of engagement

**What makes a bad talking point (filler):**
- "Markets have been volatile" with no link to the client's portfolio
- Restating a holding's YTD performance with no context or implication
- Generic product mentions not tied to the client's situation
- Information the client already has and does not need RM input on

**Topic classification:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Portfolio** | Performance, allocation, concentration, upcoming events |
| **Relationship** | Pending commitments, concerns raised, life events, mandate review |
| **Action Item** | Something requiring a decision, instruction, or follow-up |

### Phase 4 — Actions

List what needs to happen before or after this meeting. Actions should be specific, owned, and where possible, time-bound.

**Format each action as:**
- **Action:** [what]
- **Owner:** RM / Client / Both
- **Deadline:** [date or trigger]
- **Linked to:** [the observation or topic that generated it]

## Identifying What Has Changed Since the Last Meeting

The most valuable function of meeting prep is surfacing **delta** — what is different now vs the last meeting.

**To identify delta:**
- Compare current portfolio snapshot to last-meeting snapshot if available
- Highlight positions added, removed, or resized
- Note any changes to client's stated objectives, risk tolerance, or personal circumstances
- Flag market or sector developments that affect the client's holdings specifically

If a last-meeting snapshot is unavailable, state this explicitly. Do not fabricate a baseline. Use market context to approximate relevance where appropriate.

---

## Connecting Portfolio Movements to Client Concerns

Clients do not think in basis points. They think in outcomes: "Am I on track?", "Should I be worried?", "Is there anything I need to do?"

When a portfolio move is material enough to discuss:
- Translate the magnitude into a dollar-equivalent impact on their portfolio
- Connect it to the client's stated goal where possible (income, growth, capital preservation)
- Offer context for why the move happened (company-specific vs sector-wide vs macro)
- Indicate whether it warrants action, monitoring, or no response

Avoid presenting movements in isolation. A -8% move in a name is different context if it is a 1% holding vs a 15% holding. Always anchor to portfolio weight.

---

## Handling Data Gaps

Missing or incomplete data is common. Handle it gracefully and transparently.

- If CRM interaction history is unavailable: note it explicitly; default to portfolio-focused prep
- If portfolio data is partial: work with what is available and flag missing positions clearly
- If last meeting date is unknown: do not estimate; frame observations as "current state" rather than delta
- If client risk profile is not on file: note it and flag it as a relationship action item
- Never fill a data gap with an assumption presented as fact
- Use "Not available" (not N/A) for data that could not be retrieved

---

## Output Structure

The final meeting prep document follows this order:

1. **Client Summary** — one-line context (name, segment, risk profile, meeting purpose, date)
2. **Since Last Meeting** — brief delta summary (2–4 bullets)
3. **Portfolio Snapshot** — key positions and current allocation summary
4. **Talking Points** — 3–5 labeled discussion topics, each with type tag and action flag
5. **Likely Client Questions** — 2–3 questions the client may raise, with suggested RM context
6. **Pre/Post-Meeting Actions** — action list with owner and deadline

Target: RM should be able to scan and internalize this document in under 3 minutes.
